# Chennai Metro Rail (CMRL) — Knowledge Base for RAG

---

## 1. Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Stand in queue.
- Limit your baggage to 25 kgs weight and size not exceeding 80 cm length x 50 cm breadth x 30 cm height.
- Stand on the left side of the escalator.
- Move away from escalators immediately after use.
- Show your tickets to metro personnel when required.
- Take care of your valuables.
- Offer your seat to ladies with infants, pregnant women, and elderly passengers.
- Note: Parking is at owner's risk.
- Do not litter — use the dustbins.
- Make way for the physically challenged.
- Contact the Station Controller or Customer Care for assistance.

### Don'ts
- Don't travel without a valid CMRL ticket.
- Don't share your card or token with another person on the same journey.
- Don't cross the yellow line on the platform.
- Don't step onto the metro tracks.
- Don't jump over the ticket barrier.
- Don't try to force the train doors open.
- Don't deface or put up posters inside the metro station or train.
- Don't carry firearms or knives.
- Don't smoke.
- Don't consume eatables inside station premises or on trains.
- Don't bring pets inside metro premises or trains.

## 2. Items Not To Carry

Passengers are prohibited from carrying the following on the metro railway.

### Dangerous materials
- Explosive substances (risk of explosion or fire)
- Compressed, liquefied, or pressure-dissolved gases
- Petroleum and other inflammable liquids
- Inflammable solids
- Oxidizing substances
- Poisonous (toxic) substances
- Acids and other corrosives
- Radioactive substances
- Weapons, arms, and ammunition
- Any Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear, and Enhanced Conventional Weapons (CBRNE) posing risk to passengers or property

### Offensive materials
- Dried, coagulated, or decomposed blood (human or animal)
- Corpses
- Carcasses of dead animals or birds
- Bones (including bleached/cleaned bones)
- Human skeletons
- Parts of human bodies
- Portable radio devices that risk interfering with the radio communication network or signaling systems

### Animals
- Live animals or birds are not allowed.
- **Exception:** Security personnel on duty may bring a sniffer dog for security purposes.

## 3. Overstay Penalty for Metro Travel

- **Same station entry & exit:** If a passenger stays in the paid area beyond **20 minutes**, a penalty of **Rs. 10/hour** (max **Rs. 100**) is charged at exit.
- **Different station entry & exit:** If a passenger stays in the paid area beyond **120 minutes**, a penalty of **Rs. 10/hour** (max **Rs. 100**) is charged at exit.

## 4. Metro Railways (Carriage and Ticket) Rules, 2014 — Chennai Metro Railway Rules

> Note: The official PDF linked above is a scanned/image-based document with no extractable text layer, so it could not be parsed directly. The text below is the same regulation (Chennai Metro Railway (Carriage and Ticket) Rules, 2014, notified under the Metro Railways (Operation and Maintenance) Act, 2002) sourced from a public legal database mirroring the official Gazette notification.

**Preamble:** Issued by the Central Government in exercise of powers under Section 32 of the Metro Railways (Operation and Maintenance) Act, 2002 (Act 60 of 2002).

**Rule 1 — Short title and commencement**
- May be called the Chennai Metro Railway (Carriage and Ticket) Rules, 2014.
- Comes into force on the date of publication in the Official Gazette.

**Rule 2 — Definitions**
- "Act" means the Metro Railways (Operation and Maintenance) Act, 2002.
- "Authorized" means authorized by the metro railway administration.
- Undefined terms take the meaning given in the Act.

**Rule 3 — Carriage of goods or luggage**
- Passengers may carry only baggage of personal belongings not exceeding **60 cm x 40 cm x 25 cm** in size and **15 kg** in weight, unless prior approval is obtained from the metro railway administration.
  *(Note: CMRL's own "Do's and Don'ts" page above states a higher limit — 25 kg, 80 cm x 50 cm x 30 cm — which reflects a later amendment/practice; the 2014 Rule text itself specifies 15 kg / 60x40x25 cm.)*

**Rule 4 — Prohibition against carriage of dangerous and offensive goods**
1. Dangerous materials banned: explosives; compressed/liquefied/pressurized gases; petroleum and inflammable liquids; inflammable solids; oxidizing substances; toxic substances; acids/corrosives; radioactive substances; weapons, arms and ammunition; and any other material the administration declares dangerous.
2. Offensive materials banned: dried/coagulated/decomposed blood (except packed fresh human blood for medical use); blood-stained material (except accident victims' clothing); corpses; animal carcasses; bones (except bleached/cleaned bones); fertilizer/manure; oily rags; decayed animal/vegetable matter; human ashes; human skeletons; parts of human bodies; and any other material declared offensive by the administration.
3. Live animals/birds are banned, except security sniffer dogs on duty.
4. Officials may inspect containers suspected of holding dangerous/offensive material.
5. Officials may remove from the metro any person carrying such material.

**Rule 5 — Exemption for authorized personnel**
- Members of the Armed Forces, State Police, Para-military forces, NCC, and other uniformed government forces may carry their authorized weapons/arms/ammunition while on duty, subject to furnishing proof of identification.

**Rule 6 — Application of other Acts and rules**
- These rules don't override the Explosives Act 1884, Explosives Rules 1983, the Arms Act 1959, or the Arms Rules 1962.
- A single safety matchbox or a pocket cigarette gas lighter is permitted.

**Rule 7 — Diseases declared infectious and contagious**
- No person suffering from the following may travel: Cerebro-Spinal meningitis, chicken-pox, cholera, diphtheria, measles, mumps, scarlet fever, typhus fever, typhoid fever, whooping cough, or any other disease declared contagious/highly infectious by the Central or State Government.
- **Exception:** A non-infective (closed) leprosy patient may travel with a certificate from a registered medical practitioner confirming they are non-infective.

**Rule 8 — Particulars of ticket**
1. A ticket is issued on payment of fare, governed by the Business Rules of the Metro Railway Administration.
2. Ticket types:
   - Single journey ticket (token or contactless smart card)
   - Stored value card (validity as authorized)
   - Group ticket for group journeys
   - Any other ticket type authorized by Business Instructions issued from time to time

## 5. Store Value Pass (SVP) — Online QR

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Initial deposit | Rs. 50 |
| Minimum recharge | Rs. 50 |
| Maximum top-up | Rs. 3,000 |
| Validity | 5 years from purchase or last recharge, whichever is later |
| Discount | 20% off the fare on every journey |

**Advantages:**
- No need to queue for tickets every journey.
- No physical card needed — buy via the CMRL mobile app and get a QR code for travel.
- For security, the QR code changes daily.
- The same SVP can be used to pay CMRL parking fares and get commuter parking discounts.

**Note:** If a passenger enters and exits the same station within 20 minutes, the minimum fare is deducted from the SVP balance.

## 6. Group Ticket / Family Ticket

### QR Group Ticket
- Purchased via the CMRL mobile app for a group of **20 or more passengers** (in effect since September 2020).
- Gets a **20% discount** on the normal fare.
- Children under 90 cm in height travel free, even on a group ticket (effective 22 Feb 2023).

### Single QR Family Ticket
- For a group of **2 to 5 passengers** traveling together.
- **No discount** is given on the Family QR Ticket.

## 8. Facilities for Women Passengers

- Women-only coach on every train.
- CCTV cameras across stations and trains.
- Customer Care facility available.
- Security staff posted at all metro stations.
- **Pink Squad** — special women security personnel trained in martial arts, who patrol routinely to ensure women's safety and check for male commuters traveling in the women-only coach.
- **Women's Helpline:** 155370

## 9. Facilities for Differently Abled People

Every metro station is designed for universal access from street level to platform level and inside trains, including:

- Drop-off bays with kerb ramps at street level, and kerb ramps connecting road level to footpath level near lift entries.
- Tactile signage from drop-off bays to the station lift entry, and from street-level entry through to the platform via the ticketing office (for visually challenged passengers).
- Dedicated parking spaces close to station entries.
- Entry ramps with a 1:12 slope from street to footpath to podium level.
- Dedicated low-height ticket counters at concourse level.
- Dedicated wide AFC (Automatic Fare Collection) gates for wheelchair access.
- Continuous handrails on both sides of staircases, at 900 mm height.
- Lifts from street to concourse and concourse to platform.
- Dedicated seating areas at platform level with grab rails.
- 100 mm wide warning strips, 600 mm from the platform edge.
- Low-height drinking water facilities.
- Chime and audio indication for train arrival/departure.
- Differently-abled accessible toilets at concourse/platform level, with a tactile path leading to them.
- Colour-contrasting strips at the start and end of staircases.
- Identification signage for wheelchair-accessible coach doors.
- Computer-based public announcement system.
- Long-stop buttons inside trains.
- Dedicated wheelchair space inside trains.
- Braille system and handrails inside lifts.
- Grooving provided on staircases.
- Lower-height lift control panels.
- Wheelchairs and stretchers available at the Station Control Room.
- Loop induction units installed (for hearing aid users).
- Signage at appropriate locations throughout stations.
